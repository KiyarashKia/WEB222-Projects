Intro

"The Boy, The Mole, The Fox and The Horse" is a series of deeply heartfelt conversations about the meaning of life, truth and friendship.
Basically, it is a book written by Charlie Mackesy. It is noticeable that there is an animation based on Mackesy's book which has been created on year 2022 by Apple TV and BBC. The director is Peter Baynton and it goes through all the feelings of each character, even though it is a short animation.
All four characters represent different parts of the same person. Each can be considered the true depth of a person’s real character in life. A boy who is curious and concerned; A mole who is enthusiastic; A fox who has been hurt and is disappointed in life and cannot trust easily anymore; And the horse which represents wisdom and patience of the assuming character who can be any of us, humans! And the relationship between our fears, hopes, courage and kindness.
Either reading the book or watching the animation, you will fall in love with each character as you surely can relate them to your inner, real side. We all have good and bad days; there are always ups and downs in our lives and because of them, we can be delighted, disappointed or tired. This is simply what the story is about: "There is always and still hope!"

Plot

The animation "The Boy, The Mole, The Fox and The Horse" unfolds a poignant and heartfelt journey revolving around the unlikely camaraderie of a boy, a mole, a fox, and a horse. Together, they traverse through various landscapes in the boy's quest to find a home, encapsulating a narrative filled with themes of kindness, friendship, courage, and hope, which is portrayed in a heartwarming, classically animated film setting. This tale, based on Charlie Mackesy's book, aims to resonate with viewers of all ages, instilling a narrative of compassion and hope through the adventures and bonds formed among the central characters (Apple Original Films)

Characters

Each represent feelings in all of us!

  • The Boy

    The boy is lonely and curious and is an important figurehead for the reader’s journey through the book.

    Over time, the Boy builds friendships with other characters and demonstrates the value of vulnerability and compassion when seeking to gain a greater understanding of the world.

  • The Mole

    The wise mentor for The Boy, the Mole is happy to share wisdom through insightful questions that help the reader and The Boy understand what’s happening.

    The Mole is a great friend and also loves cake! The Mole represents patience and maturity.

  • The Fox

    The reader will soon become acquainted with the third character. Initially, the Fox is hesitant to trust anyone, but gradually the Fox warms up to the other characters and opens up. The Fox represents the power of bonds and trust.

  • The Horse

    The final character – the horse is strong, but gentle, he’s the anchor that keeps the group on steady ground. The Horse often sees the bigger picture in the situation and encourages the group to confront their weaknesses.
